Why Quality Concrete Installation Matters

In Michigan's climate, concrete faces serious challenges. Temperature swings, freeze-thaw cycles, deicing salts, and moisture all take their toll. Proper installation makes the difference between concrete that lasts 30+ years and concrete that fails in 5-10:

‚úÖ Site Preparation

Removing organic material, unstable soil, and establishing proper grades prevents settling and drainage problems.

‚úÖ Compacted Base

4-6 inches of compacted gravel provides a stable foundation and allows water to drain away.

‚úÖ Air-Entrained Concrete

Microscopic air bubbles allow water to expand when freezing without cracking the concrete.

‚úÖ Proper Reinforcement

Rebar, wire mesh, or fiber reinforcement adds tensile strength and controls cracking.

‚úÖ Control Joints

Properly placed joints create weak points where cracking occurs in straight, hidden lines.

Our Concrete Installation Process

  1. Project Planning

    We assess the site, discuss your needs, and determine the right approach for your concrete project.

  2. Site Preparation

    Clear the area, remove unstable material, and excavate to proper depth for base and concrete.

  3. Base & Forms

    Spread and compact gravel base. Set forms to establish edges, elevation, and drainage slope.

  4. Reinforcement

    Install rebar, wire mesh, or specify fiber reinforcement based on project requirements.

  5. Pour & Finish

    Place concrete, level, float, and apply the specified finish. Cut control joints at proper intervals.

  6. Cure & Protect

    Apply curing compound and protect the new concrete. Provide care instructions for proper strength development.

Frequently Asked Questions About Concrete Services in Detroit

What concrete services do you offer?

We provide complete concrete services including flatwork (patios, sidewalks, pool decks), foundation slabs, garage floors, driveways, retaining walls, steps, decorative stamped concrete, and concrete repair/replacement throughout Metro Detroit.

How thick should concrete be for a patio or slab?

For residential patios and sidewalks, 4 inches is standard. Driveways and garage floors should be at least 4-6 inches. Foundation slabs for buildings require 4-8 inches depending on load requirements and building codes.

How long does concrete need to cure before use?

Concrete reaches about 70% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days. You can walk on it after 24-48 hours. Wait 7 days for vehicle traffic on driveways and 30 days for heavy loads or equipment.

Do you use reinforcement in concrete?

Yes, we use rebar, wire mesh, or fiber reinforcement depending on the application. Reinforcement significantly increases tensile strength and helps prevent cracking, which is especially important in Michigan's freeze-thaw climate.

What time of year can you pour concrete in Detroit?

Ideal concrete weather is May through October when temperatures stay above 50¬∞F. We can pour in cooler weather using blankets, heated enclosures, and accelerators, but costs increase and there are limitations in very cold conditions.

Can you match existing concrete?

We can get close, but exact matching is difficult because concrete color changes with age and weather exposure. For seamless appearance, full replacement of visible areas is often the best approach.

What areas do you serve for concrete work?

We serve Detroit and all of Metro Detroit including Dearborn, Livonia, Warren, Southfield, Royal Oak, Troy, Farmington Hills, Ann Arbor, Sterling Heights, and surrounding communities in Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties.
