Our Construction Process

  1. Pre-Construction Planning

    Finalize plans, select materials and finishes, obtain permits, and establish a detailed construction schedule.

  2. Site Work & Foundation

    Clear the lot, establish grades, excavate, and pour foundation with proper waterproofing and drainage.

  3. Framing

    Build floor, wall, and roof structures. Install windows, doors, and complete the building envelope.

  4. Mechanical Rough-In

    Install electrical wiring, plumbing, and HVAC ductwork before walls are closed. Inspections at each stage.

  5. Insulation & Drywall

    Install insulation, hang and finish drywall, and prepare surfaces for painting and finishing.

  6. Interior Finishes

    Install flooring, cabinetry, countertops, paint, and all interior trim and fixtures.

  7. Final Completion

    Complete exterior work, final inspections, touch-ups, and comprehensive walkthrough with you.

Frequently Asked Questions About New Home Construction in Detroit

How long does it take to build a new home in Detroit?

Standard new home construction takes 6-12 months depending on size and complexity. Weather can impact timelines in Michigan, particularly for foundation work in winter. Custom homes with unique features may take longer. We provide detailed schedules at project start.

How much does new home construction cost in Detroit?

New construction costs in Detroit typically range from $150-$300+ per square foot depending on finishes, features, and site conditions. A 2,000 sq ft home might cost $300,000-$600,000 or more for custom builds with premium finishes.

Do you help with permits for new construction?

Yes, we handle all permit applications and inspections for new construction projects. This includes building permits, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, and any other required permits from local municipalities.

Can I use my own architect or plans?

Absolutely. We're happy to build from your architect's plans or plans you've purchased. We'll review them for constructability and may suggest modifications to improve efficiency or reduce costs.

What financing options are available for new construction?

New construction typically requires a construction loan that converts to a permanent mortgage. We can recommend lenders experienced with construction loans and work with your financing requirements.

What warranties do you provide?

We provide a warranty on our workmanship, plus all manufacturer warranties on materials and systems. Specific terms are detailed in your construction contract. Michigan also provides certain statutory warranties for new construction.

What areas do you serve for new construction?

We build new homes throughout Metro Detroit including Detroit, Dearborn, Livonia, Warren, Southfield, Royal Oak, Troy, Farmington Hills, Ann Arbor, Sterling Heights, and surrounding communities.
