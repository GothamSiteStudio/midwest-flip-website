Frequently Asked Questions About Kitchen Remodeling in Detroit

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Detroit?

Kitchen remodel costs in Detroit vary based on scope and materials. A minor refresh typically starts around $15,000-$25,000, while a complete renovation with custom cabinets and premium countertops can range from $35,000-$75,000+. We provide free detailed estimates for every project.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

Most kitchen remodels in Detroit take 4-8 weeks depending on scope. A cabinet and countertop replacement might take 2-3 weeks, while a complete gut renovation with layout changes typically requires 6-10 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline before work begins.

Do I need a permit for kitchen remodeling in Detroit?

In Detroit and most Metro Detroit communities, permits are required for electrical, plumbing, and structural work. We handle all permit applications and inspections as part of our service, ensuring your project is code-compliant.

Can I stay in my home during a kitchen remodel?

Yes, most homeowners stay during kitchen remodels. We help you set up a temporary kitchen space and work to minimize disruption. We maintain a clean worksite and communicate daily about progress and any access needs.

What's included in a kitchen remodel estimate?

Our estimates include labor, materials, permits, debris removal, and all specified work. We provide detailed breakdowns so you know exactly what you're paying for. Any potential additional costs are discussed before they occur.

Do you help with kitchen design?

Yes! We work with you on layout planning, material selection, and design decisions. While we're builders, not interior designers, we have extensive experience creating functional, beautiful kitchens and can recommend design professionals if needed.

What areas do you serve for kitchen remodeling?

We serve Detroit and all of Metro Detroit including Dearborn, Livonia, Warren, Southfield, Royal Oak, Troy, Farmington Hills, Ann Arbor, Sterling Heights, and surrounding communities in Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties.
