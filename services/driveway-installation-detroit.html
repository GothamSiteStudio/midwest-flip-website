Signs You Need a New Driveway

Not sure if repair is enough, or if replacement is needed? Here are indicators that full replacement is the better investment:

  • Extensive Cracking: Multiple large cracks wider than 1/4 inch or spreading in a web pattern
  • Heaving or Sinking: Sections that have lifted or settled, creating uneven surfaces
  • Drainage Issues: Water pooling on the surface or draining toward your foundation
  • Potholes and Spalling: Surface breaking apart, creating holes and rough areas
  • Age: Concrete driveways over 25-30 years or asphalt over 15-20 years often need replacement

Our Driveway Installation Process

We've refined our process to deliver quality driveways efficiently:

  1. Site Evaluation

    We assess your existing driveway, drainage patterns, soil conditions, and discuss your preferences for material and design.

  2. Removal & Excavation

    Old driveway is broken up and hauled away. We excavate to proper depth and remove unstable soil.

  3. Base Preparation

    Gravel base is spread in layers and mechanically compacted to create a solid, stable foundation.

  4. Form Setting

    Forms are installed to define edges and ensure proper slope for drainage away from structures.

  5. Installation

    Concrete is poured, leveled, and finished, or asphalt is laid and compacted. Pavers are set in pattern.

  6. Finishing & Curing

    Control joints are cut, edges are finished, and proper curing time is allowed before use.

Frequently Asked Questions About Driveway Installation in Detroit

How long does a new driveway installation take?

Standard concrete driveway installation takes 3-5 days including excavation, base preparation, pouring, and initial curing. Asphalt driveways can be completed in 2-3 days. Paver driveways typically take 4-7 days depending on size and pattern complexity.

Which is better for Detroit: concrete or asphalt driveways?

Both work well in Detroit when properly installed. Concrete lasts 30+ years and handles freeze-thaw well with proper joints. Asphalt is more affordable, handles salt better, and can be resealed. We help you choose based on your budget, aesthetic preferences, and maintenance willingness.

How soon can I drive on my new driveway?

For concrete driveways, you can walk on it after 24-48 hours, but should wait 7 days before driving on it, and 30 days before parking heavy vehicles. Asphalt can typically be driven on after 3-5 days depending on weather conditions.

Do you remove the old driveway?

Yes, our driveway installation includes complete removal and disposal of your existing driveway, excavation, proper base preparation, and installation of the new surface.

What time of year is best to install a driveway?

Late spring through early fall (May-October) is ideal in Michigan. Concrete needs temperatures above 50Â°F for proper curing. Asphalt needs warm temperatures for proper compaction. We can work into cooler months with appropriate precautions.

Can you widen my existing driveway?

Yes, we can add width to your existing driveway for extra parking space or easier maneuvering. We'll match the new section as closely as possible or discuss full replacement if a seamless look is important.

What areas do you serve for driveway installation?
